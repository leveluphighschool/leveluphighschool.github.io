1. ENVIRONMENT

Use your surroundings to help you remember where your classes are

The school gym

Finding your way around any new environment is always difficult.

Here are some of the best ways to adjust yourself:

  • Use landmarks to help find your way around school (e.g. B-block is the brick building that is to the right of the tuck shop).
  • Know where signposts which point to the direction of blocks of classrooms are.
  • Ask lots of questions to prefects, teachers and friends about where certain buildings are, tuckshop, administration office, nurse's office and of course toilets!
  • Use your phone! Set your school timetable and a map of the school (can be downloaded from most schools’ websites) as either your lock or home screen background to help you remember the order of your classes and where they are in the school
2. CLASSES

Doing fun experiments in the Chemistry lab!

Notes from class to copy into my book later...

The main difference between intermediate and high school is having to carry your bag around to a new class each period with different teachers and students.

Here are my top tips to help out with adjusting:

  • If you have a locker, make sure you have the right gear for your classes by visiting it before school or during break
  • Make good friendships with your teachers and classmates - classes are so much more fun when you know at least one person in the room. Remember your classmates are in the same boat so you don't need to be scared of talking to them.
  • Follow your teacher’s instructions: you may find yourself working in potentially dangerous rooms such as science labs or workshops (e.g. remember to keep your bags underneath tables, wear safety goggles)
3. HOMEWORK / ASSESSMENTS

My friend, Kelly smiling through the pain of getting assignments done on time

Don't be the person who forgot the homework because they didn't write it down somewhere. Use your diary!

Unfortunately, you'll get a LOT more homework compared to in intermediate school.

But it's not the end of the world!

Here are some tips you can use to organise yourself:

  • Separate a huge project into small jobs you can do each day.

    For example, say you have an essay due on Friday.

    On Monday, come up with a plan for it.
    On Tuesday write the introduction.
    On Wednesday write the first body paragraph and repeat and repeat!

    That is so much easier than sitting at your desk with what seems like a mountain of an assignment to get through.

  • Use a diary to plan out your weeks - aim for a balance between social and study time
  • Make the most of lunchtime and before/after school - can visit the school library or go to tutorials for a subject for help
  • Ensure you know exactly what is required for a project - ask teachers if confused or have any problems
4. ADVICE FOR THE FIRST DAY

Here are my top tips to get through your first day stress-free!
  • Pack your lunch and bag the night before
  • Bring a pen and refill paper just in case you need to be writing.
  • Make sure you know how you are getting home (e.g. what bus you are catching)
  • Ask questions
  • Enjoy yourself!
