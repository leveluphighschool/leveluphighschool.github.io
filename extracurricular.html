EXTRA CURRICULAR

Extra curricular activites are a HUGE part of life in high school.

They are an awesome way to make new friends with people who have the same interests, as well as give you opportunities to do something completely out of your comfort zone!

Keep reading to get an idea of what activites you might be excited in doing when you start high school.

~ SPORTS ~

This changes from school to school, however the huge range of sports you can try at high school include Basketball, Athletics, Hockey, Netball, Waterpolo, Equestrian, Archery and Orienteering to name a few.

As well as the competitive competition, many schools will let you get together with your friends, and form a team for the 'social' or non-competitive competition.

At the end of the day it's all about having fun so don't worry about how badly you can kick a ball.

Just get into it!



~ MUSIC ~

Many schools allow you to have music or singing lessons to help you learn a new instrument or develop your voice.

There are also plenty of orchestras and choirs that you can audition for based on your ability as well!

Premier music groups have the opportunity to compete in nation-wide competitions such as KBB Music Festival and the Big Sing, and win awards for outstanding performances - these are great to talk about when you are applying for leadership roles or even for university scholarships in a few years.



~ ARTS ~

High school is full of opportunities to show off your creativity in not only drawing, but also through Dance and Drama.

Joining dance troupes and the school production are both rewarding experiences and you don't have to be a performer to do so.

Behind the scenes, you can help with set and prop designs as well as costuming for it.


~ HUMANITIES ~

Joining humanitarian groups such as Envirogroup (concerned about the environment), Amnesty International (human rights organisation) and UNICEF (concerned about well-being of children in developing countries) or even debating are so helpful in opening your eyes to issues present in the world today.

They are a space where you can develop your own opinions on these topics, and participate in social actions or fundraising in order to make the world a better place.

Still not keen?

What about starting your own club? Even if your passions lie in reading, chess or knitting, if you pitch your idea well enough to the deans, there's nothing stopping you from doing so!
