EXTRA CURRICULAR

Extra curricular activities are a huge part of life in high school.
~ SPORTS ~

This changes from school to school, however the huge range of sports you can try at high school include Basketball, Athletics, Hockey, Netball, Waterpolo, Equestrian, Archery and Orienteering to name a few. But, there's always a space for you, no matter how badly you can kick a ball. As well as the competitive competition, many schools will let you get together with your friends and form a team for the 'social' or non-competitive competition.



~ MUSIC ~

Many schools allow you to have music or singing lessons to help you learn a new instrument or develop your voice. Also don't forget about the orchestras and choirs that you can audition for based on your ability as well! The top music groups have the opportunity to compete in nation-wide competitions such as KBB Music Festival and the Big Sing, and win awards for outstanding performances.



~ ARTS ~

High school gives you a lot of chance to show off your creativity in not only drawing, but also through Dance and Drama. Joining dance troupes and the school production are both rewarding experiences and you don't have to be a performer to do so. Behind the scenes, you can help set and prop designs as well as costuming.


~ HUMANITIES ~

Joining humanitarian groups such as Envirogroup (concerned about the environment), Amnesty International (human rights organisation) and UNICEF (concerned about well-being of children in developing countries) or even debating are so helpful in opening your eyes to the issues of the world today. They are a space where you can develop your own opinions on these topics, and participate in social actions or fundraising in order to make the world a better place.

Still not keen?

What about starting your own club? Even if your passions lie in reading, chess or knitting, if you pitch your idea well enough to the deans, there's is nothing stopping you from doing so!
